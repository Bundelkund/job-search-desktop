# Letter Forge — Question Bank

> ~35 questions across 5 parts. Ask 3–4 per turn; never dump all questions at once.
> Each question includes a *hint* (one line, italicised) — always show it; it motivates
> honest, specific answers.
>
> Questions are in English. Answers are accepted in any language.

---

## Part 1: Story & Arc (8 questions)

> *What this part reveals*: the origin and through-line of your career. The material here
> feeds your positioning paragraph and sets the authentic tone for every cover letter.

| ID | Question | Hint |
|----|----------|------|
| P1.1 | What was the moment or experience that first pulled you toward your field — not the logical decision, but the emotional spark? | *Intrinsic motivation, narrative depth, authenticity signal for hiring teams* |
| P1.2 | If you had to explain your career path to a curious 14-year-old, what story would you tell? What would you leave out? | *Self-narrative skill, ability to prioritize, awareness of identity vs. role titles* |
| P1.3 | What is the problem in the world — big or small — that you personally feel called to work on? How does your professional life connect to that? | *Purpose alignment, values-driven motivation, cultural fit signal for mission-driven orgs* |
| P1.4 | Describe a professional achievement that nobody gave you credit for but that you are quietly proud of. What did it require of you? | *Self-awareness, intrinsic reward patterns, hidden strengths not on a standard CV* |
| P1.5 | Where do you want to be professionally in 3 years? Not the polished interview answer — the honest one. | *Growth ambition alignment, retention likelihood, development path compatibility* |
| P1.6 | What are you currently learning or building outside your day job? How does it connect to your professional path? | *Growth mindset evidence, self-directed learning, passion projects as differentiators* |
| P1.7 | What did you want to be when you were 10 years old? Is there a thread connecting that childhood dream to what you do today? | *Identity continuity, self-reflection depth, narrative authenticity* |
| P1.8 | How would a colleague who worked closely with you describe what you are *really* good at — not your job title, but the specific capability? | *Peer-perceived strengths, USP from external perspective, differentiation material* |

## Part 2: Values & Motivation (7 questions)

> *What this part reveals*: what you need to do your best work, and what drives honest
> career decisions. This becomes the tone and "voice rules" layer of your profile.

| ID | Question | Hint |
|----|----------|------|
| P2.1 | Name three working conditions that are absolute non-negotiables for you — things you need to do your best work, not nice-to-haves. | *Self-knowledge, boundary clarity, probation risk factors if unmet* |
| P2.2 | Think of a time you left a job, project, or team. What was the real reason — the one you might not have said in the exit interview? | *Honest self-assessment, values clarity, pattern recognition for recurring friction* |
| P2.3 | What kind of leadership brings out your best? Describe a specific boss or manager who helped you do your finest work. What did they do differently? | *Leadership compatibility needs, management style preferences, probation success predictor* |
| P2.4 | What ethical line would you not cross, even if your employer asked you to? | *Integrity depth, values under pressure, assessment-centre ethics scenario readiness* |
| P2.5 | Why are you looking for a new role right now? Not the polished version — the real, complete answer you would tell a trusted friend. | *Transition motivation, push vs. pull factors, retention risk awareness* |
| P2.6 | What would make you turn down an otherwise perfect job offer? | *Deal-breakers, value hierarchy, negotiation priorities* |
| P2.7 | When you feel most "yourself" at work — what are you doing, and who are you with? | *Flow state indicators, team composition preferences, role design alignment* |

## Part 3: Working Style & Voice (6 questions)

> *What this part reveals*: how you collaborate, communicate, and handle pressure. This
> directly shapes the `writing_style` and `skills_matrix` fields — the voice of every
> cover letter you generate.

| ID | Question | Hint |
|----|----------|------|
| P3.1 | In a group with no clear leader, what role do you naturally drift toward? (Structurer, mediator, challenger, executor, encourager — or something else?) | *Natural team role, group dynamics contribution, assessment-centre group exercise behaviour* |
| P3.2 | How do you explain complex things to people outside your field? Give a real example of something technical you made accessible. | *Communication adaptability, audience awareness, presentation readiness* |
| P3.3 | Walk me through how you approach a problem you have never seen before — your actual, messy, real process, not the textbook answer. | *Problem-solving methodology, comfort with ambiguity, case study readiness* |
| P3.4 | What is your relationship with written communication? Does it come naturally to you, or is it a struggle? How do your messages typically sound? | *Written communication style (directly relevant to cover letter voice), daily work communication fit* |
| P3.5 | Describe your worst professional day in the last two years. What happened, how did you get through it, and what did you learn about yourself? | *Stress response, resilience, self-awareness under pressure* |
| P3.6 | What is your relationship with perfectionism? Where does it help you and where does it hold you back? | *Self-regulation insight, quality vs. speed balance, workload management style* |

## Part 4: STAR Achievement Evidence (8 questions)

> *What this part reveals*: behavioural evidence for the `achievements` and `skills_matrix`
> fields. These become the numbers and stories that make a CV and cover letter credible.
>
> **How to use**: invite free narrative first ("just tell me what happened"). Only offer the
> Situation / Task / Action / Result / Reflection breakdown if the user wants it or if
> the story needs clarifying.

| ID | Question | Competency |
|----|----------|------------|
| P4.1 | Tell me about a project where you had to rally people around a goal that was not obviously exciting. How did you create buy-in? | Leadership, influence, motivation |
| P4.2 | Describe a situation where you failed and had to recover. What did recovery look like? | Resilience, accountability, growth mindset |
| P4.3 | Tell me about a time you had to deliver results with fewer resources than you needed. What trade-offs did you make? | Resourcefulness, prioritization, pragmatism |
| P4.4 | Describe a moment where you gave someone honest feedback they did not want to hear. What happened afterward? | Courage, empathy, relational intelligence |
| P4.5 | When have you challenged a process or rule that you thought was wrong? What was the outcome? | Initiative, critical thinking, constructive dissent |
| P4.6 | Tell me about a time you made an unpopular decision. How did you handle the pushback? | Conviction, stakeholder management, decision confidence |
| P4.7 | What is the most creative or unconventional solution you have ever implemented at work? What made you think of it? | Innovation, creative thinking under constraints |
| P4.8 | Looking across your career, what is the one achievement you would most want a hiring manager to know about — the number, outcome, or impact you are most proud of? | Core differentiator, quantified peak achievement |

## Part 5: Positioning & Target Roles (6 questions)

> *What this part reveals*: how you want to be seen in the market and what roles fit you
> now. This is the direct input to your `positioning` field.

| ID | Question | Hint |
|----|----------|------|
| P5.1 | In one sentence, how would you complete this: "I help [who] do [what] so that [outcome]"? | *Elevator pitch, core value proposition, positioning statement raw material* |
| P5.2 | What kind of organization do you do your best work in? (Size, sector, stage, culture — be specific.) | *Target company profile, fit filter for job ranking* |
| P5.3 | What role types are you targeting right now, and what makes those the right fit for where you are in your career? | *Role clarity, application focus, narrative coherence* |
| P5.4 | What do you bring to a role that most candidates at your level do not? Be specific — avoid generic claims. | *Differentiation, USP articulation, competitive positioning* |
| P5.5 | What would you need to be able to say in 12 months to feel that this next role was a success? | *Success criteria, concrete vision, probation goal alignment* |
| P5.6 | If a recruiter asked "why should we pick you over someone with a more conventional background?", what would you say? | *Narrative reframe, gap bridging, confidence under scrutiny* |

## Usage notes

- **Batch size**: 3–4 questions per turn. Show the next batch only after receiving answers.
- **Skip rule**: any question can be skipped. Note the ID and move on.
- **STAR stories** (Part 4): accept free text; structure only if helpful.
- **Pause**: user can stop at any point. Progress lives in-conversation; note where to resume.
- **Part navigation**: user can jump to any part by saying "Part 3" or "P4.2".
